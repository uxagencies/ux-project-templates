# Request for Proposal (RFP)
### [Project name] — [Your company]

> [!NOTE]
> **Why this document exists.** A good RFP gets you proposals you can actually compare, from agencies who self-select *out* if they're a bad fit. A vague one gets you ten wildly different documents, padded budgets, and the wrong shortlist. The effort here is repaid in every conversation that follows.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **An RFP is not a design brief.** This is how you *choose* a partner; the [brief](design-brief-template.md) is how you *align* on the work after. Don't specify the solution here — describe the problem and let the proposals show you how each agency thinks.

> [!TIP]
> **Before you send it:** invite **3–5 agencies**, not ten. A focused shortlist signals you're serious and respects everyone's time — agencies put real effort into proposals only when they believe they have a genuine shot.

> [!NOTE]
> Agencies do better work — and price more accurately — when they understand who you are. Give them enough to feel the shape of the company.

- **Company:** [Name, what you do, who you serve]
- **Size / stage:** [e.g. Series A, 40 people / public / bootstrapped]
- **Website & relevant links:** [URLs]
- **What prompted this project now:** [The trigger — a new strategy, a falling metric, a deadline, a raise]

## 2. The opportunity

> [!TIP]
> Lead with the problem, not the deliverable. *"Our onboarding loses 60% of signups in the first session"* tells an agency far more than *"we need a new onboarding flow."*

- **The problem or opportunity:** [In plain language]
- **Who it affects and how:** [Users, the business, the team]
- **What we've already tried:** [Be honest — what worked, what didn't]
- **What happens if we do nothing:** [The cost of the status quo]

## 3. Goals & success metrics

> [!IMPORTANT]
> State outcomes, not outputs. "Ship a redesign" is an output. "Increase trial-to-paid from 4% to 7%" is an outcome — and it's how you'll judge whether the project worked.

| Goal | How we'll measure it | Current baseline | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| [e.g. Reduce checkout support tickets] | [Tickets/week tagged 'checkout'] | [120/week] | [< 50/week] |
| | | | |

## 5. Scope & deliverables

> [!TIP]
> Describe the work as phases and questions, not a fixed feature list. If you already know the exact solution, you may not need an agency — you need a contractor.

- **In scope:** [e.g. discovery, IA, UX/UI design, design system, handoff specs]
- **Explicitly out of scope:** [e.g. development, copywriting, maintenance — naming these prevents padded bids]
- **Expected deliverables:** [Wireframes, prototypes, design files, documentation]
- **Existing assets to build on:** [Brand, design system, research, codebase — note what's usable]

## 8. Budget

> [!WARNING]
> Hiding your budget wastes everyone's time and produces proposals scoped to a number nobody can confirm. A range is enough — good agencies will tell you what's achievable within it.

- **Budget range:** [e.g. €40,000–60,000] *or* **engagement model:** [fixed price / time & materials / retainer]
- **Anything affecting how budget is released:** [Phased approval, fiscal-year boundaries]

## 9. What to include in your proposal

> [!TIP]
> Asking everyone for the same structure is the single biggest thing you can do to make proposals comparable.

- [ ] Your understanding of our problem (in your own words)
- [ ] Proposed approach and phases
- [ ] Relevant case studies — ideally similar *problem*, not just similar industry
- [ ] The team who'd actually work on this (not just the names that win pitches)
- [ ] Timeline with key milestones
- [ ] Pricing, broken down by phase
- [ ] Assumptions and what you'd need from us
- [ ] How you handle [research / accessibility / handoff — whatever matters most]

## 10. How we'll evaluate

> [!NOTE]
> Sharing your criteria — and roughly how you weight them — produces sharper proposals and signals you're a thoughtful client worth winning. (See the [cheat sheet](CHEATSHEET.md) for green flags vs. red flags when reading responses.)

| Criterion | Weight |

| Relevant experience & case studies | [25%] |
